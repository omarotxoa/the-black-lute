Soleá comes from the spanish word "Soledad" which means "Solitude." It is said that the Soleá is the mother of all palos. Although it is not the oldest palo, it is held by Flamenco players as one of the most important and fundamental to learn. It is a style of flamenco with 12 beats. I say adapted for beginners, because you won't find simple Soleá's like this in recorded materials and the tempo is really slow for a Solea. Most soleá recordings you can hear online (spotify and the like) will have very fast, very complex arpeggios and falsetas as well as fast rasgueados and other tecniques that the beginner cannot execute without rigorous practice. Even something as simple as staying in rythm with the 12-beat compas at its usual 90bpm is difficult for the beginner (hence why I played at my own pace in the video).
