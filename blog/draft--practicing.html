Making the shift to improvising and creating

Posted on October 13th, 2021

When I first started playing guitar, I was the type of player that collected a lot of bits of music. Intros to songs I knew, cool little solo's or licks, etc.

Recently, I've been throwing that out and trying to approach the guitar as an instrument to make my own music, not just play someone else's stuff. I know that may seem obvious to some, but it never occured to me that I could create my own music.

For whatever reason, I just had it in my head that I wasn't 'good enough' for that. Or that the guitar was just my hobby, and why even bother trying to make music if there was no possible way I had the dedication that other professionals did.

Maybe its just the kind of thought process that pops up in kids. Maybe that's why you get so many little groups of kids - the rockers, the rappers, the ska rockabilly types, etc. Its a weird state of mind where there's only room for one type of music. I think whatever it is in our brains that does that also passes over to other things - jobs, careers, etc. You label yourself, and in doing so, you restrict yourself.

So now that I look back on it it kind of makes sense to me why I only focused on predominantly rock music and always someone elses stuff. I had mentally fucked myself into thinking I would never be able to create something good because I just didn't have the talent - so I restricted myself into playing other people's stuff.

My dive into flamenco completely removed that odd mental barrier. I was initially attracted to flamenco because of its dissonant sounds and fast rasgueado/picado technique - it seemed to me that FLamenco was a kind of Metal on Acoustic.
